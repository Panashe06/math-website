Area of Triangles

If you want to make a triangle into a rectangle, you’ll first need to split another triangle of the same size into two and fit it’s pieces along their hyponeuses. This fact shows us multiple things, most importantly that the area occupied by two congruent triangles are equal that of 1 rectangle. This conclusion leads to another. Which is that 1 triangle is half the area of a rectangle: Since the area of a rectangle is L*W, the area of a triangle can be represented by the formula, A= (1/2)BH, Where B is the length of the rectangle and H is it’s height
