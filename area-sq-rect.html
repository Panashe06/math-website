The area taken up by a square or rectangle is simply calculated by mulitplying it’s length by it’s width:
L is a variable that represents the legnth of the rectangle (it’s longest side), while W represents the width of a rectangle (the shorter side). If you are finding the area of a square then the two values are the same. Think of area as taking a line that is “W” units long, and stacking “L” amount of them on top of each other
