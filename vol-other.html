Other volume formulas

The volume of a cylinder is V = πhr^2, where “h” represents the height and “r” the radius:

The volume of a pyramid is V = (⅓)BH, where the “B” is the area of the base of the pyramid (found by using the formula A=LW) and “H” represents the height (line perpendicular to the base):

The volume of a cone is V = (⅓)(π)(r^2)(h), where “r” is the radius (length from the center of the circle to any point on it) and “h” is the height (line perpendicular to the base):

The volume of a sphere is V = (4/3)πr^3, where “r” is the radius (length from the center of the circle to any point on it):
