What are they?

A linear equation is an equation whose terms are all powered by 1. A linear equation should have a constant rate of change. When the x y pairs are graphs as this happens, it creates a line. An example is 4x-1=y.

If we substitute x in this equation for 1, then we can solve this equation to find the value of y when we give x a value of 1:

  1. 4(1)-1=y
  2. 4-1=y
  3. 3=y
