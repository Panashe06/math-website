Volume of Cubes and Rectangular Prisms

A cube is a 3 dimensional object with all four of it’s sides being made up of squares. Squares are polygons with 4 equal sides connecting to one another.

The formula for solving this is V=l*w*h. Where L is the length of the base, w is it’s width, while height represents how long the line perpendicular to the shape is.

Another way to think of it is multiplying the area of the base by the height to see how much space is in the shape when multiple bases are stacked on top of each other, as represented in the formula, V=a*h.

It is key to highlight that any side of a prism can be a base, and the height is always the length of a side perpendicular to (90 degrees of) the base you are using, but the best practice is to treat the base as the longest side.

For the volume of a cube, the same formulas work, but another quicker way is cubing one side of the cube, like in the formula V = s^3, where represents a side’s length.
