Slope intercept form

In the previous example, we graphed an equation that was written in slope intercept form. The formula for which is, mx + b = y. In this equation, the variable “m” represents the slope of the graph. A graph’s slope is the ratio of it’s change in y value to it’s x value. This change in the axis is y values of an ordered pair subtracted from the x values of those same ordered pairs. The order in which it is subtracted is the greatest ordered pair by the one lowest in value:

The variable “b” is the constant (the nonchanging object) of the equation. In slope intercept form it will be the y intercept of the line. The y-intercept shows where the line intersects with the y-axis when x=0, so when plugged in we get, m(0)+b=y--->b=y
